As I scoured the web for specific photos of even more specific things I came across (again) this beautiful Janie Hirsch article on Decorati. The Atlanta designer manages to create spaces that look "collected" and "perfectly staged" at the same time. The result is relaxed elegance - my favorite look.

So as I continue to seek out the ideal lamp tables and oversized rugs I'll always keep looking to images of professionals whose work I admire. It helps me stay on course and reminds me why the quest for the idea piece is of such importance. The lesson: keep learning, keep looking, keep seeking, keep at it. It's always worth it in the end AND throughout the process. Creative fields require you (yes, require you) to continually learn and evolve. The great names in your field didn't get there by lagging behind or refusing to improve their craft. They committed to continuous growth and improvement. If you find yourself swimming in a sea of new information, consider yourself lucky: you've stumbled upon new things to learn!
